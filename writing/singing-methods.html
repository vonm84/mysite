Singing methods & the voice — where do I sit?

What this piece is: the accessible essay version of the "what does each method think the voice IS" question. The honest job is NOT to summarise 15 methods — that's a trap. It's to take ONE spine, the liberating facts, and YOUR alternative, and write it warm. One essay, one spine. (If it wants to become a series later, fine — decide as you write.)

Raw material lives in: sources/Singing Methods and the Voice_ A Critical Survey of Vocal Pedagogy's Hidden Ontology.pdf(.md) — a scannable dossier, deliberately not an essay. Mine it; don't transcribe it.

The one spine (the hook — from the research, to be put in plain words)

"A voiceless player playing their own voice like an instrument." Most Western vocal teaching quietly treats your voice as an instrument you operate from the outside. But then — who is the "you" doing the operating, and why is that "you" assumed to be silent?
This is the single strongest, most original hook. A feeling anyone can grasp even with every academic name (Cavarero, Schenker, "devocalization of logos") stripped out. Strip them out.

The liberating facts (mine these — keep the citations, lose the jargon)

"In tune with the piano" is one local tuning system, not a law of nature. Gamelan, mbira — whole traditions never agreed to the piano. ("As many scales as there are gamelans.") This is your old §8 (the "too nerdy for lessons" one) — THIS is its home.

Speech is already song. Diana Deutsch's speech-to-song illusion: repeat a spoken phrase and it tips into melody. The speech/song border is perceptual and porous — backs your "speech is already tonal" claim.

The "non-singer" is made, not born. ⚠ The amusia / 1.5% numbers that usually carry this point are exactly what you're sceptical of — DON'T lean on them here. That argument gets its own room (writing/tone-deafness.html). Here, make the "made, not born" case some OTHER way (history, culture, the "vulgar voice" verdict, photoshop-for-the-voice) and link out to the numbers piece.

Decisions to make before publishing (the live tensions — your call)

Do you keep the word "natural"? (Linklater/NVN do — and inherit "a true voice under the blocks" essentialism.) Can you state your alternative as just uniqueness + relation, no "natural"?

Vocal HEALTH is the one acoustic-ideal you probably can't dethrone (you can damage the instrument). Name it openly as the legitimate floor, so the piece can't be dismissed as romantic anti-technique.

Autotune is your cleanest villain — but some artists use it expressively. Concede that, and the polemic gets stronger, not weaker. Do you?
